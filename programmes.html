Free Technology Education for All

We offer four core programmes designed to meet learners and educators where they are. All our programmes are completely free for participants, thanks to the generosity of our volunteers, sponsors, and partners.

Flagship Programme

After-School Code Clubs

Weekly sessions | 2 hours per week | 15-20 learners per club

Our Code Clubs are designed to introduce learners to the world of programming in a fun, supportive, and project-based environment. Over 12 weeks, students learn the fundamentals of web development, including HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, building their own websites and simple games.

What learners will achieve:

  • Build and publish their first website
  • Learn problem-solving and logical thinking skills
  • Collaborate with peers on coding projects
  • Present their work at our graduation showcase
  • Receive a certificate of completion

Current Locations:

Soweto Hub (Mondays & Wednesdays) | Tembisa Hub (Tuesdays & Thursdays) | Partner schools (varies by term)

Professional Development

Teacher Digital Training

6-week course | 3 hours per week | SACE-accredited

We believe that empowering teachers is the most sustainable way to integrate technology into education. Our 6-week training programme equips educators with the confidence and skills to use technology effectively in their classrooms, even with limited resources.

Course modules include:

  • Basic computer literacy and troubleshooting
  • Using educational apps and online resources
  • Creating digital lesson plans and presentations
  • Internet safety for learners
  • Integrating technology across the curriculum
  • Managing a classroom with limited devices

School Holidays

Holiday Tech Camps

5-day intensive | Full-day sessions | Includes robotics

During school breaks, we offer immersive technology camps that go beyond coding. Students explore robotics, app development, digital design, and 3D printing in a fun, hands-on environment. Each camp culminates in a project showcase for parents and community members.

Camp activities include:

  • Building and programming LEGO robots
  • Creating mobile app prototypes
  • Digital art and graphic design basics
  • Guest speakers from the tech industry
  • Team challenges and competitions
  • Field trip to a tech company (when available)

Donation Programme

Device Donation Drive

Refurbished computers | For partner schools | Corporate partnerships welcome

Access to devices remains one of the biggest barriers to digital literacy. Through our Device Donation Drive, we collect, refurbish, and distribute computers and tablets to under-resourced schools and learners who need them most.

How it works:

  • For donors: Donate working laptops, desktops, or tablets (any age). We handle data wiping and refurbishment.
  • For schools: Apply to receive devices for your computer lab or classroom.
  • For learners: Qualifying students can receive a refurbished laptop for home use.

Impact to date:

Over 350 devices distributed • 12 school computer labs established • 200+ learners with home access

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our programmes.

Are all programmes really free?

Yes! All our programmes are completely free for participants. We are funded by corporate sponsors, grants, and individual donations.

How can my child join a Code Club?

Complete the enquiry form on our website. If your child's school is a partner, they can join directly. Otherwise, we will add them to our waiting list for hub-based clubs.

Do I need my own computer?

No. We provide all necessary equipment at our hubs and partner schools. For home learning, we can provide a device if needed.

Can my school partner with EduBridge?

Absolutely! Contact us to discuss partnership opportunities. We are always looking to expand to new schools.
